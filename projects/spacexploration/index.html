SPACE EXPLORATION: HOW EXPLORATION HAS EVOLVED TO GO BEYOND EARTH'S ATMOSPHERE

DESCRIPTION

Space exploration is the process of discovering and studying the celestial objects and phenomena that exist beyond the Earth's atmosphere. It involves the use of technology and scientific methods to gather data and information about planets, moons, asteroids, comets, stars, galaxies, and other celestial bodies.

IS IT SUSTAINABLE

Currently, with the rockets emitting so much fuel and massive amounts of CO2 gasses, it's not as sustainable for the environment. Once rocket scientists think of different ways to enhance rockets to make it environmentally sustainable, we will have to stick with our good old rockets for a while.

HISTORY

In 1957, the Soviet Union launched the world's first satellite, Sputnik 1, into orbit, marking the beginning of Space Age. Throughout the 1960s and '70s, the United States and the Soviet Union engaged in a space race to the Moon, with the American Apollo program ultimately achieving the first lunar landing in 1969. In the following decades, spacecraft were sent to explore other planets in our solar system, including Mars, Venus, Jupiter, and Saturn. Since then, space exploration has continued to advance, with the development of satellite telescopes like the Hubble and the launch of the International Space Station in 1998. Private companies like SpaceX have also entered the space industry, leading to new developments in space tourism and commercial space exploration.
